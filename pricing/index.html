FAQ

Common questions

Can I customise the skin's HTML and use it commercially?
Yes — fully. All skins are MIT-licensed. Download, modify, brand, and ship in your own product with no fees and no attribution required. The only thing to keep in sync is the config.js contract so your device and skin stay talking.
Does the free tier really have no time limit or sign-up?
Yes — MIT licence, forever, no account needed. The code lives on GitHub. Download it, modify it, ship it. No strings.
What do I need to provide for a custom skin?
Share your config.js — or just the JSON keys your device broadcasts. A rough sketch of the layout is helpful but not required. We handle the rest and deliver a single HTML file.
What if my device doesn't speak WebSocket?
The runtime supports both WebSocket and HTTP polling — your device just needs to serve a JSON endpoint at any interval. The integration support tier can walk you through setting that up on any common embedded platform.
Will the skin work on a device with no internet?
Yes. The skin is a single self-contained HTML file. All fonts and scripts are either embedded or fetched once and cached. Once deployed to the device's filesystem or SD card, it works fully offline.
Can I use a custom skin commercially?
Yes. Once delivered, the skin belongs to you. You can deploy it on as many devices as you like, including in commercial products.
How long does a custom skin take?
Typically 3–5 working days from receiving your JSON contract. More complex layouts or multiple views may take longer — we'll confirm before starting.
